Quail Eggs — Benefits & Product Details

Quail eggs are a nutrient-dense specialty product with growing demand in gourmet, health and hospitality markets. We supply carefully graded, cleaned and packed quail eggs for retail, foodservice and industrial clients.

Nutritional Highlights

  • Vitamins: Rich in Vitamin A, B12, Riboflavin (B2), Niacin, and Pantothenic acid — supporting vision, energy and metabolism.
  • Minerals: Iron, Phosphorus, Selenium — essential for oxygen transport, bone health and antioxidant defence.
  • Protein: High biological-value protein with a balanced essential amino-acid profile — ideal for growth and recovery diets.
  • Fats: Beneficial fatty acids in small quantities; overall low per-serving caloric load.

Health Benefits

  • Boosts Immunity: Antioxidants & selenium support immune health.
  • Improves Vision: Vitamin A helps maintain photoreceptor function and night vision.
  • Supports Brain Function: B12 and choline support neurotransmitter synthesis.
  • Skin & Hair: Nutrients aid cellular repair, maintaining skin and hair health.
  • May Assist Allergy Management: Contains ovomucoid proteins studied for anti-allergenic effects.

Packaging & Export Options

  • Retail packs (10-count in recyclable clamshells) with cold-chain logistics.
  • Bulk packaging for foodservice — hygienic cartons with temperature control.
  • Private label and OEM packaging available for large buyers.

Quality & Certifications

We facilitate HACCP-compliant handling, phytosanitary documentation, and lab test certificates on request. Quality checks at source and pack-house ensure consistent sizing and hygiene.

Ceylon Cinnamon — Sri Lanka's Signature Spice

Ceylon (Cinnamomum verum) is distinct from cassia: it delivers a delicate, multi-layered aroma and lower coumarin levels — making it the premium choice for food manufacturers, specialty bakers and health-conscious buyers.

Why Sri Lankan Cinnamon is Unique

  • Authentic botanical variety (Cinnamomum verum) with delicate sweet-spicy notes.
  • Low coumarin content compared to cassia; preferred for food & nutraceutical use.
  • Smallholder cultivation with traditional processing yields premium quills.
  • Traceability available — batch-level origin data for premium buyers.

Applications

  • Retail spice jars and sachets
  • Food manufacturing (bakery, confectionery)
  • Essential oils and natural extracts

Packaging & Quality

Hand-graded quills, moisture-controlled packing, and private-label options for bulk or retail buyers. We support regulatory documentation for EU and Middle East exports.
